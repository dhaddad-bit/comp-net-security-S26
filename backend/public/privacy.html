Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-04-22

Social Scheduler ("the app", "we") is a web application that helps users find overlapping availability within groups and negotiate meeting times. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, and how it is stored and protected. The app is operated for educational and non-commercial use.

1. Google User Data We Access

When you sign in with Google, the app requests the following OAuth scopes:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.readonly — read-only access to your Google Calendar events.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email — your Google account email address.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.profile — your basic profile (name, Google account ID).

Social Scheduler requests read-only access to Google Calendar (calendar.readonly scope) solely to compute overlapping availability within groups you create or join. We do not edit, create, delete, or share your calendar data.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • Display your calendar events to you in the app's dashboard.
  • Compute overlapping free time among members of a group you belong to.
  • Let group members propose and respond to meeting-time "petitions".
  • Identify your account and associate it with your groups and responses.

We do not use your Google user data for advertising. We do not sell it. We do not transfer it to third parties except as required to provide the core features listed above (for example, using Google's own APIs to read your calendar), or as required by law.

3. Limited Use Disclosure

Social Scheduler's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

4. Data We Store

  • Your Google account ID, email, display name, and a username you choose.
  • A cached copy of your calendar list and upcoming calendar events, kept so the app can compute availability without re-fetching every request.
  • Groups you belong to, petitions you create or respond to, and invite tokens.
  • Encrypted OAuth refresh and access tokens for Google (see Section 5).
  • A server-side session cookie identifying your logged-in session.

All data, including encrypted tokens and cached calendar events, is processed and stored on servers located in the United States. By using Social Scheduler, you consent to your data being transferred to and processed in the United States.

5. How Your Tokens Are Protected

OAuth refresh tokens and access tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM authenticated encryption before they are written to the database. The encryption key is stored outside the database in server configuration, so a database read alone cannot yield usable credentials. Tokens are never written to application logs.

Access tokens are short-lived and refreshed automatically when they are within five minutes of expiry. Session cookies are marked HttpOnly, Secure (in production), and SameSite=Lax.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account:

  • Your encrypted Google tokens are destroyed.
  • Your cached calendar events and calendar list are deleted.
  • Your user record, group memberships, and petition responses are deleted.

You may also revoke the app's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page, which will immediately invalidate our stored tokens.

Cached calendar events are kept temporarily to compute availability without re-fetching on every request. This cache is automatically refreshed and stale data is purged periodically, or immediately upon account deletion.

You have the right to request access to the personal data we hold about you, or request that we correct any inaccuracies. Since all calendar data is synced directly from Google, corrections to calendar events must be made within your Google Calendar account.

7. Data Sharing

Within a group you join, other group members can see your availability (as free/busy time blocks) for the purpose of scheduling. They cannot see the titles, descriptions, or attendees of your calendar events unless you explicitly create an event in the app to share.

We do not share your Google user data with any third party other than Google itself, and we do not use it for advertising or machine-learning model training.

8. Security

The app is served exclusively over HTTPS. OAuth flows use a per-request CSRF state parameter validated on callback. Tokens are encrypted at rest as described in Section 5. No security system is perfect; please report any suspected vulnerability to the contact below.

9. Children

Social Scheduler is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

10. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
